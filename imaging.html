Diagnostic Imaging

Diagnostic imaging plays a supportive role in evaluating severe hyperacusis and noxacusis.

1. Purpose of Imaging

  • Rule out secondary causes.
  • Identify structural abnormalities in auditory or neurological pathways.
  • Guide management for atypical, asymmetric, or progressive symptoms.

2. Common Imaging Modalities

a. MRI

  • Preferred for unilateral or neurologically complex cases.
  • Detects vestibular schwannoma, vascular compression, demyelinating disease, brainstem lesions, SSCD.

b. CT

  • Best for bony and middle-ear abnormalities.
  • Detects SSCD, perilymph fistula, otosclerosis, ossicular defects.

c. Functional / Research Imaging

  • fMRI and PET show increased activation in auditory and limbic regions.
  • Supports central gain enhancement and emotional-auditory network dysfunction.

3. Clinical Indications

  • Unilateral or asymmetric symptoms
  • Auditory pain triggered by normal sound levels
  • Associated hearing loss or tinnitus
