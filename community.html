Community & Resources

On this page, you’ll find links to communities and resources for those affected by hyperacusis, noxacusis, middle ear myoclonus (MEM), tonic tensor tympani syndrome (TTTS), and tinnitus. These include online support groups, educational websites, and other helpful tools to connect, learn, and find guidance.

Discord

Hyperacusis Support Discord Server — A community of over 400 members who openly share their experiences and treatment outcomes, offer encouragement, and celebrate each other’s progress.

Tinnitus Labs Discord Server — A community of over 1,900 members focused on tinnitus and the treatment of tinnitus using a DIY adaptation of the Susan Shore device—originally developed at the University of Michigan but not yet commercially available. Members share treatment results and discuss related conditions such as hyperacusis, noxacusis, and TTTS.

Facebook

Hyperacusis Surgery Talk — A community of over 1,300 members dedicated to the surgical management of hyperacusis, with a primary focus on the Silverstein procedure. Members exchange experiences, outcomes, and insights.

Middle Ear Myoclonus (MEM) Support Group — A community of over 2,000 members focused on middle ear myoclonus, where people share experiences, coping strategies, and treatment results.

Tonic Tensor Tympani Syndrome (TTTS) Support Group — A community of over 2,300 members dedicated to tonic tensor tympani syndrome (TTTS). Members connect to exchange personal experiences, discuss treatment approaches, and support one another in managing symptoms.

Forums

When seeking hyperacusis advice on Reddit and other forums, it’s important to remain wary—many posts are inaccurate or misleading, and inexperienced sufferers can take a single success story as a guarantee, which may lead to unrealistic expectations.
