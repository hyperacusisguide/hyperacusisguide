Community & Resources

On this page, you’ll find links to communities and resources for those affected by hyperacusis, noxacusis, middle ear myoclonus (MEM), tonic tensor tympani syndrome (TTTS), and tinnitus. These include online support groups, educational websites, and other helpful tools to connect, learn, and find guidance.

Discord

Hyperacusis Guide Discord Server — This is our official server and the easiest way to reach us. Our personal experience with hyperacusis, noxacusis, and tinnitus allows us to assist newly affected individuals in finding effective management strategies and treatments.

Hyperacusis Support Discord Server — A community of over 400 members who share their personal experiences, treatment outcomes, and insights. Hyperacusis Guide highly recommends this server.

Tinnitus Labs Discord Server — A community of over 1,900 members primarily focused on the treatment of tinnitus using a DIY adaptation of the Susan Shore device, originally developed at the University of Michigan but not yet commercially available. Members also discuss other auditory conditions, such as hyperacusis and noxacusis, and share the latest research.

Facebook

Hyperacusis Surgery Talk — A community of over 1,300 members dedicated to the surgical management of hyperacusis, with a primary focus on the Silverstein procedure.

Middle Ear Myoclonus (MEM) Support Group — A community of over 2,000 members focused on the medical and surgical management of Middle Ear Myoclonus (MEM).

Tonic Tensor Tympani Syndrome (TTTS) Support Group — A community of over 2,300 members dedicated to the medical and surgical management of Tonic Tensor Tympani Syndrome (TTTS).

Forums

When seeking tinnitus and hyperacusis advice on Reddit and other forums, it’s important to remain wary. Many posts are inaccurate or misleading, and inexperienced sufferers can take a single success story as a guarantee, which may lead to unrealistic expectations.
